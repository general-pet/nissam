Known K9K / J11 Issues

💀
HP Fuel Pump / Injector Failure
The K9K HP fuel pump is extremely sensitive to dirty or late-changed fuel filter. Failure costs €1,000–2,500. Use only quality diesel.
⬤ Monitor — next fuel filter at ~172,000 km
🔥
EGR Valve & Cooler Clogging
EGR clogging causes rough idle, power loss and increased DPF load. Common on K9K from 80k km+. Clean every 40–60k km.
✓ Done — EGR recently cleaned
💨
DPF Clogging / Ash Build-up
DPF passive regen requires sustained highway speeds. City-only driving leads to premature blocking. Ash (irreversible) accumulates over lifetime.
⬤ 40% ash level — highway driving profile is ideal
⚙️
Timing Belt Tensioner Bearing
Tensioner bearings on K9K can fail before the belt wears out. Replacement is mandatory with every belt change. Failure = bent valves.
✓ SKF kit fitted July 2023 @ 116,315 km
💧
Water Pump (with Timing Belt)
Water pump should always be replaced with the timing belt. If skipped, pump failure at ~150–180k km forces a full belt job again.
⚠ Verify if water pump was changed in the 2023 belt job
🔩
Alternator OAP Decoupler Wear
The one-way alternator pulley (OAP) wears on K9K, causing belt squeal or rattle on cold start / shutdown. Very common at 100–130k km.
✓ Replaced ~145,000 km
🛞
Control Arm Bushings & Ball Joints
Front lower control arm bushings crack and ball joints wear from ~100k km. Often missed when only struts are replaced. Common pregled fail point.
⚠ Original — inspect on lift at pre-inspection visit
🛢️
Oil Consumption (K9K)
K9K engines can consume up to 0.5 L/1000 km when worn. Check dipstick every 3,000–4,000 km. Always top up with 5W-30 ACEA C3/C4.
⬤ Check dipstick regularly between services
